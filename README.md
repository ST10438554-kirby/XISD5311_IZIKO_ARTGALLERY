# XISD5311_IZIKO_ARTGALLERY
## Presentation Cue Cards
### IZIKO Museum Mobile Application – Presentation

## Slide 1: Introduction

Good day everyone. We are Core3 Systems, and today we will be presenting our Iziko Museum Mobile Application. The purpose of this application is to provide local and international users with an interactive digital museum experience that promotes South African art and culture.

## Slide 2: Problem Statement

The problem we identified is that visitors currently need to physically visit the museum to experience its collections. This limits access for international tourists, researchers, and young people. Since many young people prefer digital platforms, we proposed a mobile application to make the museum experience accessible from anywhere and anytime. It makes it easier for the museum itself  because it currently relies on in person tours and paper which limits the outreach engagement. The app will be turning a one channel museum into a globally accessable culture platform.

## Slide 3: Proposed business Solution 

Our solution is an Android mobile application developed using Kotlin. The application will allow users to explore artworks, learn about artists, access educational content, and engage with museum exhibitions digitally. We aim to bring the full museum experience to the user with behind the screens content .

## Slide 4: System Architecture

Our system follows a three-tier client-server architecture. The client layer consists of the Android application. The application layer processes business logic such as authentication and searching. The database layer uses MySQL and Firebase to store and manage information.

## Slide 5: Key Features

The app includes several important features such as browsing artworks, viewing artist information, listening to audio guides, scanning QR codes, taking virtual tours, and participating in quizzes. Users will also be able to save their favourite artworks.

## Slide 6: Target Users and Value

Our target users are international tourists, students, young audiences, researchers, and museum visitors. The application adds value by promoting South African cultural heritage globally and making art more accessible and engaging. It extends the museum beyond its 4 walls to international audiences and remote visitors. The app builds a digital record of engagement the museum can use to plan future exhibitions.

## Slide 7: User Requirements

Users should be able to register and log in, search and browse artworks, save favourites, watch and listen to educational videos ,view exhibitions, and receive notifications. These requirements ensure a user-friendly and interactive experience.

## Slide 8: Prototype Pages

Our prototype consists of several pages including a Home Page with museum history, an Explore Artworks page, an Artists page, an Exhibitions and Events page, a Quiz page, and a Visit Planner.

## Slide 9: Budget Overview

The estimated project budget is R7 557.50. This was calculated using the minimum hourly rate and the amount of hours spent on our app . The largest portion of the budget is allocated to development because it is the most time consuming phase of the project.

## Slide 10: Database Overview

Our database contains more than twelve tables, including User, Artwork, Artist, Category , gallery, User - activity which are action logs for reporting and Favourite tables. The database is normalized to Third Normal Form to reduce redundancy and ensure data integrity. 

## Slide 11: Future Innovative Features

To make the application more innovative, we plan to include multi-language support, Virual Reality features, an AI Art Guide chatbot, and gamification through badges and challenges.

## Slide 12: Conclusion

In conclusion, the Iziko Museum Mobile Application will provide a digital companion for museum visitors, increase accessibility for tourists and young people, and promote South African art worldwide. Thank you for listening, and here is our system prototype.
